Solar System Details

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System. It's a massive ball of hydrogen and helium undergoing nuclear fusion, providing light and energy to all the planets.

Mercury

The smallest and innermost planet, Mercury is a rocky world with extreme temperatures. Its surface is heavily cratered, similar to Earth's Moon.

Venus

Often called Earth's sister planet due to similar size, Venus has a toxic atmosphere and extreme greenhouse effect making it the hottest planet.

Earth

Our home planet is the only known world to harbor life. It has one natural satellite, the Moon, and is the largest of the rocky planets.

Mars

The Red Planet has polar ice caps, vast canyons, and the largest volcano in the solar system. It's the most explored planet besides Earth.

Jupiter

The largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter is a gas giant with a Great Red Spot and dozens of moons. Its powerful magnetic field is the strongest among planets.

Saturn

Famous for its spectacular ring system, Saturn is a gas giant with numerous moons. Its rings are made mostly of ice particles and rock.

Uranus

An ice giant that rotates on its side, Uranus has a unique blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It has faint rings and many moons.

Neptune

The windiest planet, Neptune is the last ice giant with a deep blue color. It has a dynamic atmosphere with dark spots and the strongest winds in the solar system.
